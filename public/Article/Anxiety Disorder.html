Overview

Anxiety disorders are among the most common mental health conditions, affecting millions of people worldwide. While occasional anxiety before a big exam or event is normal, anxiety disorders cause ongoing distress and interfere with everyday functioning.

Common Types of Anxiety Disorders

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Chronic, uncontrollable worry about everyday matters such as health, work, or family.
  • Panic Disorder: Repeated panic attacks ‚Äî sudden surges of intense fear, often accompanied by a racing heart, sweating, or dizziness.
  • Social Anxiety Disorder: Intense fear of being judged, embarrassed, or rejected in social or performance situations.
  • Specific Phobias: Extreme fear of a particular object or situation (e.g., heights, spiders, flying).

üí¨ Causes and Risk Factors

  • Genetic factors: Family history of anxiety or mood disorders.
  • Brain chemistry: Imbalance in neurotransmitters like serotonin and norepinephrine.
  • Personality traits: High sensitivity or perfectionism increases vulnerability.
  • Stressful experiences: Trauma, loss, or major life changes.
  • Health conditions: Thyroid issues, heart arrhythmias, or chronic illness can trigger anxiety.
  • Substance use: Caffeine, drugs, or alcohol can worsen symptoms.

1. Professional Treatment

2. Self-Help & Lifestyle Changes

  • Maintain regular physical activity ‚Äî exercise reduces anxiety naturally.
  • Get enough sleep and maintain a consistent schedule.
  • Limit caffeine, nicotine, and alcohol.
  • Eat balanced meals and stay hydrated.

3. Mindfulness & Support

  • Use mindfulness or journaling to observe anxious thoughts without judgment.
  • Stay connected with supportive family and friends.
  • Join anxiety support groups online or locally.
