A group of mental health conditions involving intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) performed to relieve anxiety.

Overview

Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders (OCRDs) are characterized by unwanted repetitive thoughts and behaviors. In the DSM-5, OCD was separated from anxiety disorders and grouped with conditions involving body image concerns, repetitive habits, or control difficulties.

  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  • Body Dysmorphic Disorder (BDD)
  • Hoarding Disorder
  • Trichotillomania (Hair-Pulling Disorder)
  • Excoriation (Skin-Picking Disorder)

üß© Causes and Common Factors

  • Genetic influences: Family history of OCD or anxiety.
  • Brain differences: Irregularities in serotonin and brain circuitry.
  • Personality traits: Perfectionism or high sense of responsibility.
  • Stressful experiences: Trauma or significant life events.
  • Environmental factors: Learned behaviors or reinforcement patterns.

üí™ How to Overcome or Manage

1. Professional Treatment

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Especially Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP), which helps reduce compulsive behaviors.
  • Medication: SSRIs (e.g., fluoxetine, sertraline) help regulate serotonin levels.
  • Therapy for BDD and Hoarding: Combines CBT with motivational interviewing to improve awareness and control.

2. Self-Help & Lifestyle Strategies

  • Practice mindfulness and grounding exercises.
  • Avoid excessive reassurance or compulsive checking.
  • Maintain structure in daily routines (sleep, diet, exercise).
  • Stay away from alcohol and drugs.
  • Join support groups ‚Äî sharing helps reduce shame and isolation.

3. Family and Social Support

  • Educate loved ones about OCD, BDD, and hoarding behaviors.
  • Offer patience and empathy rather than criticism.
  • Encourage seeking therapy and celebrate progress.
