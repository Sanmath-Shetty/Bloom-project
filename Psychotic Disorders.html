Schizophrenia

  • Genetics: Family history of schizophrenia or mood disorders
  • Brain Chemistry: Imbalance of dopamine, serotonin, or glutamate
  • Stressful Life Events: Trauma, loss, or abuse
  • Substance Use: Especially cannabis or hallucinogens
  • Brain Structure Changes: Abnormalities seen in MRI studies

üí™ How to Overcome or Manage

1. Medical Treatment

  • Antipsychotic medications: Control delusions, hallucinations, and thought disturbances.
  • Mood stabilizers or antidepressants: For schizoaffective disorder.
  • All medications must be prescribed and monitored by a psychiatrist.

2. Psychotherapy

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Helps challenge distorted thoughts.
  • Family Therapy: Improves communication and family understanding.
  • Social Skills Training: Builds confidence and daily functioning.

3. Lifestyle & Self-Help

  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule and balanced diet.
  • Avoid alcohol and recreational drugs.
  • Keep a structured daily routine.
  • Practice journaling or mindfulness for stress management.

4. Support & Rehabilitation

  • Join peer support groups to reduce isolation.
  • Participate in vocational or educational rehabilitation programs.
